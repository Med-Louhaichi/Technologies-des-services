ABOUT THE SCHOOL
Main informations about the school

Higher School Of Communication Of Tunis

Founded in 1998, the Higher School of Communications of Tunis (Sup'Com). is the main training school for telecommunications engineers in Tunisia. Affiliated with the University of Carthage, the Higher School of Communications of Tunis (Sup'Com) occupies the first places for the national competition for access to engineering schools in Tunisia. Beyond its national and international influence, Sup'Com is recognized for the excellence of its academic training and the high level of competence of its graduates and researchers. An elite school, Sup'Com also benefits from a dual supervision of the Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research and the Ministry of Information and Communication Technologies.

Our trainings
Meet our amazing trainings

  • Professional Master in Operational Cyber Security

    This training is distinguished by its character co-constructed between the Higher School of Communications of Tunis (SUP'COM), the Higher Institute of Technological Studies in Communications of Tunis (Iset'Com) and the national IT security agency. (ANSI). Theoretical lessons reinforced by practice will be provided as part of this training, crowned by professional certifications.

  • Engineer training

    The duration of training at the Higher School of Communications of Tunis is 3 years sanctioned by obtaining the "National Diploma in Telecommunications Engineering". The first and second years of study comprise, each, 28 weeks of teaching, one week of language training and 5 weeks dedicated to professional training. The 3rd year comprises 14 weeks of teaching and 15 weeks reserved for the end of studies project.

  • Continuing education

    Sup'Com has a continuing education program for telecommunications technicians and engineers wishing to complete their training while continuing their professional activities. The school has set up qualifying training programs adapted and personalized according to the needs of companies. The courses are given in the evening from 5.30 p.m. to 8.30 p.m. for training courses ranging from 3 months to three years.
